About ICCT-SD 2026

The International Conference on Intelligent Computational and Communication Technologies for Sustainable Development (ICCT-SD 2026) serves as a premier platform for researchers, academicians, industry experts, and policymakers to share insights and innovations at the intersection of intelligent computation, advanced communication systems, and sustainable development. The conference emphasizes how emerging technologies such as Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), the Internet of Things (IoT), and Big Data Analytics can drive progress toward achieving the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).


Through discussions, research presentations, and case studies, ICCT-SD 2026 aims to explore the transformative role of technology in addressing global challenges related to health, society, environment, and industry. By fostering collaboration among academia, industry, and government sectors, the conference seeks to catalyze sustainable technological advancements that contribute to a more inclusive, resilient, and sustainable future.

Objectives

  • To promote interdisciplinary research integrating intelligent computational and communication technologies with sustainability-driven applications.
  • To provide a global forum for sharing innovative ideas, research outcomes, and practical implementations supporting the United Nations’ SDGs.
  • To facilitate collaboration among researchers, professionals, and policymakers for addressing real-world challenges in health, environment, and social well-being through technology.
  • To encourage the development of smart systems, secure networks, and data-driven solutions that contribute to sustainable industrial and societal growth.
  • To inspire innovation and partnerships that translate emerging technologies into actionable strategies for global sustainable development.

The City, Ludhiana.

About Ludhiana

Ludhiana, often called the Manchester of India, is the largest city in Punjab and a major industrial hub of northern India. Known for its vibrant textile industry, hosiery exports, and entrepreneurial spirit, Ludhiana blends tradition with progress. Beyond its economic prowess, the city is rich in Punjabi culture — from lively folk dances and sumptuous cuisine to warm hospitality. The Sutlej River runs along its northern edge, offering a serene contrast to the city’s bustling life. Whether you’re walking through old bazaars, exploring Gurudwaras, or enjoying a glass of lassi at a roadside dhaba, Ludhiana promises an experience full of heart and energy.

Destinations Around Ludhiana

  • Gurudwara Dukh Niwaran Sahib – A sacred Sikh shrine known for its peaceful atmosphere and historical importance.
  • Punjab Agricultural University Museum – A glimpse into Punjab’s rural heritage, traditional crafts, and agricultural evolution.
  • Nehru Rose Garden – A sprawling urban park home to over 1,600 varieties of roses, ideal for picnics and evening strolls.
  • Phillaur Fort – A historical fort built by Maharaja Ranjit Singh, showcasing Sikh military architecture and British influences.
  • Rakh Bagh Park – A beloved recreational spot featuring lush greenery, a toy train, and spaces for families to unwind.
  • Hardy’s World – Punjab’s famous amusement and water park, perfect for thrill-seekers and family fun.
